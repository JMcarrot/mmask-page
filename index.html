Abstract

State-of-the-art approaches in universal video segmentation often rely on a decoupled “segment, track, then refine” paradigm. Performance gains are typically sought by adding extra modules or auxiliary losses, which increases architectural complexity. In this work, we shift the focus from model optimization to the training strategy and propose M-Mask, a unified and trainable parameter-free Multi-dimensional Masking strategy that systematically improves segmentation across three key dimensions: spatial, temporal, and object.

The core principle of M-Mask is to train the model on masked inputs while supervising it with the original, complete ground truth. This discrepancy compels the model to infer the missing information by leveraging surrounding context, which inherently enhances contextual awareness and discriminative feature extraction. M-Mask applies this principle across three dimensions: First, Spatial Patch Masking (SPM) randomly occludes image patches. Second, Temporal Consistency Masking (TCM) removes tubelet regions across frames, guiding the model to exploit motion continuity for stable instance association. Finally, Object Confidence Masking (OCM) dynamically prunes background-related queries before cross-attention, purifying spatiotemporal context and improving foreground precision.

M-Mask is model-agnostic, parameter-free, and adds zero inference overhead. Extensive experiments demonstrate its generality and effectiveness. Notably, M-Mask delivers substantial gains on the challenging OVIS dataset, improving AP by 5.0%, 1.5%, and 0.4% under different architectures, and establishes new state-of-the-art results on both OVIS and VSPW.

Method overview

Overview of M-Mask: multi-dimensional masking along spatial (SPM), temporal (TCM), and object (OCM) dimensions.

SPM

Spatial patch masking on RGB/feature maps — random occlusion with full-GT supervision.

TCM

Temporal tubelet masking — encourages motion-consistent instance association.

OCM

Query-level masking before cross-attention — suppresses background-dominated queries.
